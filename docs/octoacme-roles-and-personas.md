# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers shape the product experience by translating user needs into intuitive designs. They collaborate with Product and Project Managers to define requirements, create design mocks and user flows, and validate usability through testing.

### Responsibilities
- Work with Product Managers to clarify user requirements and acceptance criteria
- Create wireframes, design mocks, and interactive prototypes
- Conduct usability testing and gather user feedback
- Ensure designs meet accessibility and brand standards
- Collaborate on design systems and component libraries

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible user experiences
- Reduce user friction and improve task completion rates
- Ensure design consistency across product features

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Managers and Developers
- User research findings and usability test results
- Collaboration on design specs and handoff documentation

### Example Interactions
- **With Developers**: Provides design specifications, clarifies interactions, and validates implementation against designs during code reviews
- **With Product Managers**: Collaborates on user stories, refines acceptance criteria based on user research insights
- **With QA**: Partners on usability validation and accessibility testing to ensure designs are implemented correctly

---

## DevOps Engineers

### Role Summary
DevOps Engineers enable efficient, reliable build and deployment pipelines through automation, infrastructure management, and monitoring. They bridge development and operations to ensure fast, secure, and stable releases.

### Responsibilities
- Build and maintain CI/CD pipelines for automated builds, tests, and deployments
- Manage infrastructure, environments, and cloud resources
- Implement monitoring, logging, and alerting systems
- Ensure security, compliance, and disaster recovery in deployment processes
- Optimize build times and deployment reliability

### Goals
- Reduce deployment time and increase release frequency
- Maintain high system reliability and uptime
- Ensure secure and compliant deployment practices

### Typical Communication
- Incident response and postmortem reviews
- Infrastructure planning with Project Managers
- Automation updates and pipeline improvements

### Example Interactions
- **With Developers**: Collaborates on automating release workflows, troubleshoots build failures, and provides environment configurations
- **With Project Managers**: Coordinates release schedules, communicates deployment risks, and provides infrastructure capacity estimates
- **With QA**: Sets up test environments, enables automated testing in CI/CD, and supports performance testing infrastructure

---

## Business Analysts

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge technical and business stakeholders by gathering requirements, analyzing workflows, and translating business needs into actionable technical solutions. They ensure alignment between business objectives and implementation.

### Responsibilities
- Gather and document business requirements from stakeholders
- Analyze current processes and identify optimization opportunities
- Create functional specifications and process flow diagrams
- Validate that solutions meet business needs and objectives
- Facilitate communication between business and technical teams

### Goals
- Ensure technical solutions deliver measurable business value
- Reduce ambiguity in requirements and minimize rework
- Enable data-driven decision making through analysis

### Typical Communication
- Requirements workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Business case documentation and ROI analysis
- Process maps and functional specification reviews

### Example Interactions
- **With Product Managers**: Provides detailed business context for features, helps prioritize backlog based on business impact analysis
- **With Developers**: Translates business requirements into technical specifications, clarifies edge cases and business rules
- **With Project Managers**: Supports scope definition, helps estimate business impact of schedule changes, and validates project outcomes against business goals
- **With Stakeholders**: Gathers requirements, communicates project progress in business terms, and validates acceptance criteria
